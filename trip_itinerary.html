Philadelphia Dining

Philadelphia offers a range of culinary experiences, from classic cheesesteaks to award‑winning fine dining. Here are some recommended spots for each meal.

Breakfast & Brunch

  • Café La Maude – A French‑Lebanese brunch destination in Northern Liberties. Open daily for breakfast/brunch/lunch from 8 AM to 4 PM【110372502358022†screenshot】; walk‑in only and BYOB.
  • Middle Child – A casual luncheonette inspired by Philly hoagie shops. Hours: Tue–Fri 9 AM–3 PM and Sat–Sun 8 AM–3 PM, with breakfast served until 11 AM【203906859337778†L30-L35】.
  • Dutch Eating Place – A Pennsylvania‑Dutch stall in Reading Terminal Market, famous for blueberry pancakes. Open Mon–Wed 8 AM–3 PM and Thu–Sat 8 AM–4 PM; closed Sundays【670382631424857†L122-L132】.

Lunch

  • Reading Terminal Market – A historic market offering numerous food vendors. The market is open daily 8 AM–6 PM【886073634444175†L181-L183】; vendors serve everything from roast pork sandwiches to Amish baked goods.
  • Pat’s King of Steaks – Iconic cheesesteak stand credited with inventing the cheesesteak. It operates 24 hours a day【599480514432855†L159-L169】; be ready to order “wit” (with onions) or “without.”
  • Suraya (Lunch) – Lebanese restaurant and market in Fishtown. Lunch is served Wednesday–Friday from 11 AM to 2 PM【496243820181028†L22-L29】; brunch Sat–Sun 10 AM–2 PM with the market opening at 9 AM【496243820181028†L22-L29】.

Dinner

  • Zahav – Modern Israeli cuisine with a family‑style menu. The restaurant is open Tuesday–Saturday evenings 5 PM–9:30 PM【106771744205278†L130-L135】; reservations are required weeks in advance.
  • Vernick Food & Drink – Chef Greg Vernick’s American bistro in Rittenhouse Square. Dinner is served Tuesday–Saturday 5 PM–10 PM, with the bar opening at 4:45 PM【614558337439663†L66-L78】.
  • Suraya (Dinner) – The Fishtown restaurant serves Levantine dishes Sunday–Thursday 5 PM–10 PM and until 11 PM on Friday and Saturday【496243820181028†L22-L31】.

Washington D.C. Dining

Washington offers diverse dining options ranging from classic taverns to modern farm‑to‑table restaurants. Reservations are recommended for popular venues.

Breakfast & Brunch

  • Founding Farmers – A farmer‑owned restaurant serving scratch‑made dishes. Breakfast is available Mon–Fri 7 AM–11 AM; weekend brunch Sat 8:30 AM–2 PM and Sun 8:30 AM–2 PM【212854330301417†L132-L150】.
  • Blue Duck Tavern – Located in the Park Hyatt. Serves breakfast daily 6:30 AM–10:30 AM, brunch Sat–Sun 11:30 AM–2 PM, lunch Mon–Fri 11:30 AM–2 PM and dinner daily 5:30 PM–10 PM【112144130586795†L58-L69】.
  • Le Diplomate – French brasserie with a popular weekend brunch. Brunch is served Sat–Sun 9:30 AM–3 PM and dinner runs until midnight on Friday and Saturday【311087771589824†L129-L137】.

Lunch & Dinner

  • Old Ebbitt Grill – Historic tavern near the White House. It operates Mon–Fri 8 AM–2 AM and Sat–Sun 9 AM–2 AM, with lunch and dinner menus available until 2 AM【826374088153522†L84-L110】.
  • The Hamilton – Restaurant and live‑music venue with American cuisine. Open daily 11 AM–2 AM【736105731987737†L65-L74】; the kitchen closes at 1 AM making it ideal for late‑night meals.
  • Founding Farmers – In addition to breakfast, it offers lunch and dinner Mon–Thu 11 AM–10 PM and Fri–Sat until 11 PM【212854330301417†L132-L150】.
  • Le Diplomate – For dinner, this brasserie is open daily and serves until midnight on Friday and Saturday nights【311087771589824†L129-L137】.
  • Blue Duck Tavern – Dinner served daily 5:30 PM–10 PM【112144130586795†L58-L69】; known for farm‑to‑table cuisine.
